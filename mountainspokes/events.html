The Great Continental Divide

When:

All summer long! 'The Grand Depart' starts the 2nd week of June

Requirements:

Average ride is 25days (~110 mi/day) for men, and 29.5 days (93 mi/day) for women.

All lodging, food, and repairs must be done at publicly accessible places. No private/pre-arranged lodging or repair spots.

Cost:

Since the trip is entirely self-funded costs vary.

About the Great Continental Divide

The Great Divide Route is the world's longest off-pavement cycling route. It was tirelessly mapped over a 4 year span, and published in 1998 by Adventure Cycling Association, North America's premiere bicycle travel organization. The route is highlighted by long dirt roads and jeep trails that wend their way through forgotten passes of the Continental Divide. It travels through Canadian provinces of Alberta and British Columbia, and the United States of Montana, Idaho, Wyoming, Colorado, and New Mexico (map).
